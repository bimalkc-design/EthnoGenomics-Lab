Welcome to EthnoGenomics Lab

Bridging Traditional Knowledge with Molecular Insights for Plant Conservation & Discovery

At EthnoGenomics, we delve into the genetic architecture of medicinal and herbal plants, integrating advanced genomics with rich ethnobotanical wisdom. Our mission is to unlock the potential of plant biodiversity for sustainable utilization and conservation, with a special focus on the unique flora of the Himalayas.

About the EthnoGenomics Lab

Our mission and the researcher leading the way in plant genomics and ethnobotany.

The EthnoGenomics Lab is dedicated to advancing our understanding of plant genetics, with a particular emphasis on the medicinal and herbal flora of the Himalayas. We integrate cutting-edge molecular biology and bioinformatics with invaluable traditional knowledge to explore organellar genomes, authenticate species, and support biodiversity conservation efforts. Our work is driven by a commitment to sustainable utilization of plant resources and unlocking their full therapeutic potential.

Meet the Principal Investigator

Dr. Bimal Kumar Chetri (PhD)

Principal Investigator, EthnoGenomics Lab

Plant Genomics Ethnobotany Bioinformatics Medicinal Plants Traditional Knowledge Biodiversity
Download CV

Dr. Chetri's academic journey culminates in a **PhD from IIT Guwahati** and an **MSc in Botany from Pune University**. His research explores the intricate world of plant genetics, specifically focusing on **organellar genome mining** of herbal and medicinal plants. This involves a multidisciplinary approach, blending cutting-edge **plant molecular biology** techniques with sophisticated **bioinformatics analysis**.

A significant aspect of his work is rooted in **ethnobotany**, where he integrates valuable traditional knowledge with scientific inquiry. He is particularly passionate about the rich biodiversity of Bhutan and the Eastern Himalayas, striving to generate genomic resources that aid in species authentication, conservation, and the sustainable utilization of these invaluable natural assets.

**Expertise:** Plant Genomics, Organellar Genome Mining, Ethnobotany, Bioinformatics, Medicinal Plants, GHG Inventory.
**Teaching:** Plant Diversity, Ethnobotany, Genetics & Genomics, Plant Physiology, Bioinformatics.
**Services:** NGS Data Assembly & Annotation, Downstream Analysis, Genotyping by Sequencing, Plant Flow Cytometry, Academic Writing.

Areas for Collaboration

Our core research areas where we actively seek partnerships to bridge traditional knowledge with modern genomic science for sustainable plant resource management and discovery.

Organellar Genomics

Assembly & annotation of chloroplast and mitochondrial genomes from NGS data for phylogenetic insights and marker development.

Ethnobotany & Conservation

Integrating traditional knowledge with scientific validation for plant conservation and sustainable use.

GHG Inventory & Ecology

Developing methodologies for assessing greenhouse gas dynamics in diverse vegetation types and land-use patterns.
