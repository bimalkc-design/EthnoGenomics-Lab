About Dr. Bimal Kumar Chetri

Academic and researcher at the forefront of plant genomics and ethnobotany.

Dr. Bimal Kumar Chetri (PhD)

Principal Investigator, EthnoGenomics Lab

Plant Genomics Ethnobotany Bioinformatics Medicinal Plants Traditional Knowledge Biodiversity
Download CV

My academic journey culminates in a **PhD from IIT Guwahati** and an **MSc in Botany from Pune University**. My research explores the intricate world of plant genetics, specifically focusing on **organellar genome mining** of herbal and medicinal plants. This involves a multidisciplinary approach, blending cutting-edge **plant molecular biology** techniques with sophisticated **bioinformatics analysis**.

A significant aspect of my work is rooted in **ethnobotany**, where I integrate valuable traditional knowledge with scientific inquiry. I am particularly passionate about the rich biodiversity of Bhutan and the Eastern Himalayas, striving to generate genomic resources that aid in species authentication, conservation, and the sustainable utilization of these invaluable natural assets.

**Expertise:** Plant Genomics, Organellar Genome Mining, Ethnobotany, Bioinformatics, Medicinal Plants, GHG Inventory.
**Teaching:** Plant Diversity, Ethnobotany, Genetics & Genomics, Plant Physiology, Bioinformatics.
**Services:** NGS Data Assembly & Annotation, Downstream Analysis, Genotyping by Sequencing, Plant Flow Cytometry, Academic Writing.

EthnoGenomics Research Focus

Our core research areas bridge traditional knowledge with modern genomic science for sustainable plant resource management and discovery.

Organellar Genomics

Assembly & annotation of chloroplast and mitochondrial genomes from NGS data for phylogenetic insights and marker development.

Ethnobotany & Conservation

Integrating traditional knowledge with scientific validation for plant conservation and sustainable use.

GHG Inventory & Ecology

Developing methodologies for assessing greenhouse gas dynamics in diverse vegetation types and land-use patterns.
